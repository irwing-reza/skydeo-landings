# Shape SEO

Build an SEO brief from the user's actual outcome and audience before proposing metadata or taxonomy.

## 1. Establish the ground truth

Inspect any content, site, analytics, issue, or codebase the user has placed in scope. Resolve facts from those sources instead of asking the user to repeat them. Research current search results when competitiveness, terminology, or search intent depends on them.

Identify whether the work concerns new content, an existing page, or a site-wide taxonomy. Then start with the highest-impact unresolved decision.

This step is complete when the available evidence has been inspected and the first unresolved decision is known.

## 2. Grill one decision at a time

Ask exactly one question per turn and wait for the answer. With each question, give:

- a recommended answer based on the evidence so far;
- a brief reason and the tradeoff it makes;
- concrete choices when the decision benefits from them.

Walk the decision tree in dependency order. Resolve:

- the business outcome, conversion, and success measure;
- the audience, language, geography, and level of awareness;
- the searcher's job and dominant intent;
- the page or content type and its expected lifespan;
- the primary topic and supporting questions;
- the distinctive promise, proof, or first-hand value;
- overlap or cannibalization with existing content;
- the call to action and editorial constraints;
- the taxonomy role of categories and tags.

Follow dependencies exposed by an answer before moving to the next item. Challenge vague goals, mixed intents, interchangeable claims, and taxonomy that creates empty archive pages. When the user is uncertain, recommend a direction rather than returning the decision unchanged.

The interview is complete when every applicable item is resolved, explicitly deferred, or marked irrelevant.

## 3. Deliver the SEO brief

Synthesize one coherent recommendation:

- outcome and success measure;
- audience, market, search job, and intent;
- primary topic, supporting topics, and content promise;
- differentiators and evidence the content must include;
- recommended SEO title, H1, meta description, and slug;
- one primary category and only useful reusable tags;
- outline, internal-link opportunities, call to action, and measurement notes when applicable;
- assumptions, unresolved risks, and explicitly deferred decisions.

Keep the title, description, heading, content promise, and call to action aligned to the same intent. Prefer a few broad, durable categories. Include a tag only when it will group multiple useful items into an archive a visitor could reasonably browse; otherwise omit it. Treat tags as site taxonomy, never as meta keywords.

The brief is complete when every recommendation traces to an interview decision or cited evidence, and contradictions between goal, intent, metadata, taxonomy, and content have been resolved.
